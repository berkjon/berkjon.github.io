Blogging Reflection

Thoughts on an illustrious 9-week career

Writing my DBC blogs each week has been an interesting experience. On the positives, it has spurred me to research and learn about a handful of new topics that weren't part of our core curriculum each week. It's also been good (although probably insufficient) practice getting back into medium- to long-form writing, which I have fallen woefully out of practice with since college. Back then I considered it a strength, but six years of writing emails and presentations where bullet points and brevity reign has left me with a serious case of writers block in my blog posts. Not necessarily writers block in that I can't thing of what I want to say, but rather I can't think of how to articulate it in a way that doesn't sound like a middle schooler crafting a sentence. So the practice in that regard is appreciated.

Some aspects have been more trying, though. Perhaps not surprisingly, I've found it difficult and painful to write about things that I was forced to write about. Some topics I got to choose, but others were strictly defined, and I found myself reaching for interesting things to say where I had none. Ultimately much of what I wrote felt forced, or like a regurgitation of things I'd picked up while researching the topic.

I also felt like I had to short-change the subjects I wrote about due to time constraits. The guidlines say we shouldn't spend more than about an hour researching and writing our blog posts, but I found I couldn't even research most of the technical posts adequately in that time, let alone explain them. In almost every case with the technical posts, I was writing about something I knew nothing about an hour earlier, and the plain, superficial analysis I came up with probably reflects that. Due to time constraints I also spent no time proof reading my posts, which is where I'd typically make all the smaller changes to reconfigure sentences, choose better words, and clarify or consolidate points.

Looking forward, I absolutely want to do more blogging. That's been true for years -- I have plenty of thoughts and opinions and I've often thought I'd enjoy another outlet for them. I've just struggled to make time and insert it into my routine. But I also want to do it on topics I truly care about, where I have genuine insights and opinions to share, and where I can give both the subjects, and my own writing, the time and consideration they deserve. That is my goal for my future of blogging.
